Air Pollution

Air pollution affects human health, damages ecosystems, contributes to climate change, reduces air quality, harms wildlife, and can lead to long-term environmental and economic consequences.

Overview

Air pollution occurs when harmful substances such as gases and fine particles are released into the atmosphere from transportation, industries, and energy production. It is one of the leading environmental risks globally.

Main Causes

Industrial Emissions

Factories release pollutants from fossil fuel combustion.

Transportation

Vehicles emit harmful gases that degrade air quality.

Burning Waste

Improper waste disposal releases toxic chemicals.

Effects

Health Risks

Linked to asthma, lung disease, and heart problems.

Climate Change

Greenhouse gases increase global temperatures.

Environmental Damage

Harms ecosystems, crops, and water systems.

Take Action

Reduce emissions, conserve energy, plant more trees, use public transport or eco-friendly vehicles, minimize waste, and raise awareness about air pollution in your community.

Global Climate Change

Global climate change is closely connected to air pollution. Harmful gases like carbon dioxide (CO₂), methane (CH₄), and other greenhouse gases trap heat in the atmosphere, causing the Earth's temperature to rise.

Acid Deposition & Global Warming

Definition

Acid deposition, commonly known as acid rain, occurs when sulfur dioxide (SO₂) and nitrogen oxides (NOₓ) mix with water vapor in the atmosphere and fall to the ground as acidic precipitation. Global warming refers to the long-term increase in Earth’s temperature due to greenhouse gases.

Impacts

  • Damages forests, soil, and crops
  • Harms aquatic life in rivers and lakes
  • Corrodes buildings and infrastructure
  • Contributes to rising global temperatures

Solutions

  • Reduce fossil fuel emissions
  • Promote renewable energy
  • Use clean transportation
  • Develop IT-based monitoring systems and awareness platforms

Forest & Water Resources

Definition

Forest and water resources are essential natural systems that support life on Earth. Forests help regulate climate, absorb carbon dioxide, and protect biodiversity, while Water resources provide drinking water, agriculture support, and ecosystem balance.

Impacts

  • Deforestation increases carbon emissions
  • Loss of biodiversity and wildlife habitat
  • Water pollution affects human and aquatic life
  • Climate change disrupts rainfall patterns

Solutions

  • Promote reforestation and tree planting
  • Conserve water and reduce waste
  • Use sustainable agriculture practices
  • Develop IT-based monitoring and awareness systems
